About Us

The Kolhapur Municipal Corporation (KMC) is the civic body responsible for administering Kolhapur city in Maharashtra. Established on October 12, 1954, and later upgraded to a municipal corporation in December 1972, it manages urban planning, water supply, sanitation, and public infrastructure. The corporation governs 92 elected seats and is headed by a Municipal Commissioner.

    Recent Civic Updates

  • Water Tariffs: KMC forwarded a proposal to significantly increase municipal water tariffs to manage rising operational and electricity costs.

  • Alternate-Day Supply: The civic body shifted to an alternate-day water supply schedule due to critically low water levels at the Kalammawadi dam.

  • Commercial Development: KMC has initiated plans to develop 5 to 6 large commercial complexes through Public-Private Partnerships (PPP) to boost civic revenue.

    Key Services & Facilities

  • Property & Water Tax: Residents can check and pay municipal dues via the KMC portal.

  • Birth & Death Registration: Certificates and vital records are managed directly through the official website.

  • Public Transit: The corporation oversees the Kolhapur Municipal Transport (KMT) bus network.

  • Grievance Redressal: Residents can track complaints and check the schedules for local solid waste collection on the platform.
